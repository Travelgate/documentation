# Rates

The `HotelRatePlanNotif` message contains information about rate prices and supplements. No rate information will be returned except code, currency and status.

As explained before, Channel-X extracts the product loaded between the Buyer (inventory) and the Channel Managers (availability and rates) into our [Inventory tool](../inventory-tool) and sends it to the Buyer's system, to your system. So, in this case, Travelgate will send `HotelRatePlanNotif` requests to your system, and you will respond with either success or error.


## HotelRatePlanNotif Request

In the following examples, you will observe the requests that Travelgate will send you. All the examples presented here are [Deltas](../fullcopy-and-deltas) (smaller product requests). 

If you want to explore all possible scenarios of information that may come your way, head over to [More Request Examples](rates#more-request-examples) section; there you will find [Full Copys](../fullcopy-and-deltas) (full product details requests) examples.

:::info

Note that our system works with up to 3 types of prices: per room, per standard occupancy, and per occupancy. To learn how to calculate the price for each case, go to [Price Use Cases](rates#price-use-cases).

:::

### Price Use Cases

Our system works with up to **3 types of prices: per room, per standard occupancy and per occupancy**. Although it is unusual that a Channel Manager works with more than one, it can happen. If this is the case, the 3 price types can coexist and the system should always return the lowest one at availability response. Also when more than one price is charged for the same day, the newest price will be returned as available price.

#### **Price per Room**

When a price is charged **per Room**, it means that all occupancies allowed in the room will have the same price. If an `AdditionalGuestAmount` is charged for occupancies exceeding the standard occupancy, it will be applied.

#### **Price per Standard Occupancy** (Pax/Guest)
When a price is charged **per Pax** means, it means that the price is based on the specified number of guests. If the `NumberOfGuests` is equal to or below the standard occupancy, the returned price must be the same as the charged price. If the `NumberOfGuests` exceeds the standard occupancy, the price is calculated based on the standard occupancy price and the `AdditionalGuestAmount` charged.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>How can I calculate the final price, AmountAfterTax, taking into account the taxes?</summary>
<p>Note that there are two types of taxes (and that taxes are optional): Inclusive taxes must be included in the final price. While taxes different from inclusive or not specified, are paid directly at the hotel, they are provided for informational purposes only.</p> 
<p>Taxes are found in HotelRatePlanInventoryNotif, and prices in HotelRatePlanNotif. To calculate the total price, you'll need information from both sources.</p>
<p><b></b> If this rate plan (for which you already know the price) includes <b>inclusive</b> taxes, you'll need to add them to the price to get the total.</p> 
<p><b></b> However, if taxes are <b>different from inclusive or not specified</b>, your final price will remain as you originally knew it.</p>
</details>

<details>
    <summary>When a Supplement is added to a RatePlan, what is its purpose?</summary>
    <div>
        <div>Rate plans have only one meal plan associated with them. In the event that additional meal plan options are desired to add for the same rate plan, they are included as supplements to this rate plan.</div>
    </div>
</details>
